Wordpress, my way

Once I installed Wordpress for a first time, and didn't like what I saw: The default theme just didn't cut it. So, I made my own. The thing I learned is that I will not do it again. Unless I really have to. It can be constant uphill battle to fix some fundamentals of a good user experience. Yes, I'm looking at you hover-with-no-sense-of-location-or-state-menus. Return for time invested is not that great. The good thing is that after I have walked the walk, I know what to look for when on theme-shopping spree.

Attention reader: This article was written 2012 in comparison for twenty-eleven, which has since been updated. Though some of the points have been changed, some still apply.

Who?

I go for simple and elegant solutions. For me, high quality is matter of principle. Though my strengths lie within interaction design and technical implementation, I know a little bit of everything. Thus, I can see things in a broader perspective.

I'm pretty good on my own, but at my best I'm when teamed up with talented people from other disciplines. I believe in team efforts.
